Theatre Kid Since Birth

Laufey has mentioned in her interviews that she grew up watching a loving Golden age musicals such as Carousel, The Sound of Music, Oaklahoma!, Singing in the Rain, and An American in Paris. She witnessed and felt that those movies "were so beautiful and had these dance breaks and the dresses were floating and the colors â€“ that feeling I got from that, I just wanted to create that feeling." The influences of those childhood sounds and visuals flow through her music and gives a sense of romance and whimsy every time. It makes something loved by some enjoyable for many!

A New Sound

Laufey's music is a blend of the jazz, pop, and classical melodies found in musical culture. With her rich alto voice and majestic yet catchy melodies, her songs are a mix of authentic feeling and intention. The cocktail of familiar genres of music allow it to feel nostalgic and cozy, but with twists and lyrics that sink in and stand out from other artists of the era.

She's Too Relatable I Fear

A lot of the lyrics in Laufey's songs are catchy- but veerrrrry easy to relate to as well. The themes and topics that she covers may be on the heavier end- but the airy melodies stay in your head for days. She finds the universal topics of love, moving on, nostalgia, self-dicovery and vulnerability and weaves them into her own perspectives. Her song Falling Behind, for example, talk about the stuggle to find solidarity with others-and in life in general. Some of these lyrics are:

"The sun's engaged to the sky
And my best friend's found a new guy
I'm only getting older
I've never had a shoulder to cry on
Someone to call mine
Everybody's falling in love and I'm falling behind"

The sense of wating someone to depend on (not just in the romantic sense) and feeling like a late bloomer on a lot of life's big and wonderful moments is something I think many have experienced.

In her song Letter To My 13 Year Old Self, Laufey writes about the challanges-and even better rewards- that life will bring to her. This song emulates the feeling of confidence and pride wrapped in a warm hug- of being able to look back at all you have done, and see how far you've pushed foward, like in these verses:

"You'll grow up
And grow so confident, and
Write your story, fall in love a little too
The things you thought you'd never do
I wish I could go back and give her a squeeze
Myself at thirteen
And just let her know, know that she's beautiful"

A Multi-Instumentalist To Boot!

Laufey is known for playing instruments such as the Cello, Piano, and Guitar. (For many instrumental majors, this might seem like light-work). For me, though, those are such hard and beautiful instruemnts to learn, and she always finds time to place those intruments in her songs. Having so much talent yet staying so really connect to what and who I aspire to learn to become. The sucess that she has inspires others to trust the part of them that allows them to be comfortable with who they are, and who they wish to see themsleves become. It shows all who listen to her songs that no dream is out of reach.
